## 5. Discussion

The central aim of this study was to benchmark three complementary feature representations — CLR-transformed cell type composition, GatedStructuralCFN dependency embeddings, and scVI latent embeddings — for classifying IBD donors at the donor level, using two independent scRNA-seq cohorts with minimal leakage between donors. The results reveal that no single representation dominates across all conditions; instead, the relative advantage of each approach is tied to the biological character of the disease region under study.

### CFN versus linear models: region-specific advantages

In the SCP259 cohort (UC versus Healthy), compartment CLR with a linear SVM achieves 0.956 ± 0.061, and CFN operating on compartment-level composition (102-dimensional) achieves 0.978 ± 0.050, the best overall result in that dataset. In the Kong 2023 cohort (CD versus Healthy), the picture is more heterogeneous across intestinal regions. In the terminal ileum, CatBoost on CLR features achieves 0.967 ± 0.075, while CFN reaches only 0.811 ± 0.164. In the colon, the ranking reverses: CFN achieves 0.920 ± 0.084, exceeding the best linear baseline of 0.900 ± 0.100. This regional dissociation has a plausible biological basis. The terminal ileum in Crohn's disease shows large-magnitude, largely monotone shifts in epithelial composition — villous enterocyte depletion and goblet cell dropout — that create strongly linearly separable boundaries in composition space. Colonic mucosa in CD, by contrast, exhibits subtler and more co-dependent remodeling: macrophage subtype switching, stromal activation, and coordinated changes in lymphocyte proportions that do not resolve into a single dominant axis. The CFN dependency matrix is designed to capture exactly these co-occurrence structures, and the performance advantage in the colon is consistent with that design intent.

### CFN edge stability and the role of compositional geometry

The most methodologically consequential finding in this study is the contrast in CFN edge stability between the global and compartment formulations. Global CFN, which operates on the full 51-dimensional composition vector aggregated across all cell types, yields a cross-fold edge Jaccard of 0.026 — near-zero reproducibility. Compartment CFN, operating on composition computed separately within each biological compartment, achieves a top-20 edge recurrence of 1.0 and a sign consistency of 1.0 across folds. This difference is not a model failure; it is a consequence of the unit-sum constraint inherent to compositional data. When all 51 cell type proportions must sum to one, any perturbation in one cell type is mechanically redistributed across all others, inducing spurious negative correlations throughout the composition vector. This destabilizes the graph structure learned by the CFN, which attempts to recover a dependency network from data that has been distorted by a global constraint. Computing composition within compartments breaks the single unit-sum constraint into multiple independent constraints, each operating over a smaller and biologically coherent set of types. The result is a dependency structure that is both stable and interpretable. This finding has implications beyond this specific model: any graph-based or correlation-based method applied to compositional scRNA-seq data will be susceptible to the same artifact unless the underlying geometry — Aitchison geometry rather than Euclidean — is respected, either through compartment-wise decomposition or explicit log-ratio transformations.

### scVI latent embeddings and compartment-specific disease signal

Compartment-stratified scVI, using 60-dimensional embeddings derived from 300 cells per donor per compartment across 3,000 highly variable genes, achieves 0.931 ± 0.101 in the SCP259 cohort under XGBoost classification. This is within one standard deviation of the best CLR linear result (0.956 ± 0.061) and represents a strong result given the deliberate subsampling used to equalize representation across donors. Global scVI, averaging latent representations across all cells without compartment stratification, yields substantially lower performance (0.725). The gap between global and compartment-stratified scVI mirrors the analogous gap between global and compartment CLR composition, and points to the same underlying phenomenon: disease-relevant transcriptional variation in IBD is compartment-specific and is diluted when embeddings are computed or averaged across the full cellular landscape. Critically, the near-equivalence of compartment scVI and compartment CLR performance suggests that the primary discriminative signal is in the relative cellular makeup of each compartment, not in cell-intrinsic transcriptional differences encoded in the latent dimensions. scVI's variational latent space may compress real transcriptional heterogeneity beyond what is captured at 300 cells per donor; expanding to full cell counts with genome-wide gene sets would be needed to determine whether a latent representation advantage exists at higher resolution.

### Cross-dataset transfer and disease-specific remodeling of shared cell types

We evaluated cross-dataset transfer restricted to the four cell types shared across both annotation schemas: DC1, ILCs, Macrophages, and Tregs. Transfer from the CD cohort (Kong 2023, n=71) to the UC cohort (SCP259, n=30) using XGBoost on CLR features achieves an AUC of 0.833, while CFN transfer in the same direction achieves 0.755. Transfer in the reverse direction — UC to CD — yields AUCs of 0.503 to 0.558 across all methods, indistinguishable from chance. This strong asymmetry is informative about both dataset properties and biology. SCP259 is a single-center cohort of 30 donors with relatively homogeneous UC-associated composition patterns; models trained on it carry disease-specific composition signals that do not translate to the CD context. Kong 2023, with 71 donors spanning multiple intestinal regions and a broader phenotypic range, provides a richer training distribution. The within-dataset cross-validation AUC using the same four types is 0.883 ± 0.071 in SCP259 and 0.627 ± 0.133 in Kong, confirming that the four shared types carry strong discriminative signal in the UC context but are themselves more heterogeneous within the CD cohort. This is biologically consistent: mucosal dendritic cells and regulatory T cells in UC and CD differ in activation state, cytokine environment, and spatial localization even when assigned identical annotation labels, and a composition vector built from those labels will not fully capture those differences.

### Limitations

Several limitations constrain interpretation of these results. The SCP259 cohort contains 30 donors, which limits statistical power for CFN edge interpretation and produces wide confidence intervals in the cross-validation (± 0.050 to ± 0.130 depending on model). The scVI subsampling protocol — 300 cells per donor per compartment — was chosen to equalize donor contribution but may modestly underestimate the discriminative capacity of the latent representation at full coverage. Feature engineering in the Kong 2023 cohort applied variance-threshold filtering and rare-type exclusion, reducing composition dimensionality from 55–68 features to 32–43 depending on region; whether this filtering improved or degraded performance relative to the unfiltered feature set was not formally evaluated and represents a confound in interpreting the Kong results. Cross-dataset transfer was restricted to four shared cell types due to annotation mismatches between the two studies; this severely limits the diversity of the transferable feature space and prevents direct comparison with the full-annotation within-dataset results. A harmonized cell type atlas spanning both cohorts would enable substantially richer transfer experiments and a cleaner assessment of which cell types drive disease generalizability.

### Broader implications and future directions

This study contributes to a growing body of work using single-cell compositional features for disease classification at the donor level, including the original Smillie et al. (2019) characterization of the SCP259 cohort that established the UC compositional landscape, and subsequent work by Haberman, Boland, and colleagues applying compositional approaches to IBD patient stratification. Relative to scVI-based disease prediction approaches that operate on cell-level embeddings, this study demonstrates that donor-level classification performance from latent embeddings is strongly dependent on compartment-aware aggregation, and that composition features remain competitive — and in some configurations superior — to latent representations when constructed with appropriate geometric awareness. The primary methodological contribution of this benchmark is the donor-aware cross-validation design, which prevents cross-donor leakage in both the composition and latent embedding pipelines, and the demonstration that CFN adds measurable discriminative value over linear composition models specifically in the colon region of CD. Future directions include prospective validation of the compartment CFN feature set in an independent cohort, training scVI on full cell counts with a complete gene set to assess the ceiling of latent representation performance, extension of the classification framework to a joint UC and CD versus Healthy comparison, and application of CFN to pseudobulk expression profiles rather than composition vectors as an alternative means of encoding co-regulation structure at the donor level.
